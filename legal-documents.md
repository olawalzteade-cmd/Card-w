### Global Bank Nigeria - KYC/AML Policy

**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024

#### 1. PURPOSE
To prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes while providing excellent service to legitimate customers.

#### 2. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
We comply with:
- Money Laundering (Prohibition) Act 2011
- Terrorism Financing (Prohibition) Act 2013
- Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) regulations
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF) recommendations

#### 3. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
3.1. **Basic CDD**
   - Verify identity using government-issued ID
   - Confirm address using utility bill or bank statement
   - Validate phone number and email

3.2. **Enhanced CDD (for high-risk transactions)**
   - Source of funds documentation
   - Additional identity verification
   - Enhanced transaction monitoring

#### 4. KYC VERIFICATION PROCESS
4.1. **Document Requirements**
   - Valid National ID, International Passport, or Driver's License
   - Recent passport photograph
   - Utility bill or bank statement (not older than 3 months)
   - Bank verification number (BVN) for Nigerian residents

4.2. **Verification Levels**
   - Level 1: Basic verification (email, phone)
   - Level 2: ID verification
   - Level 3: Enhanced verification (address, BVN)
   - Level 4: Premium verification (source of funds)

4.3. **Processing Time**
   - Level 1: Instant
   - Level 2: 24 hours
   - Level 3: 48 hours
   - Level 4: 72 hours

#### 5. TRANSACTION MONITORING
5.1. **Automatic Monitoring**
   - All transactions monitored in real-time
   - Suspicious activity flagged automatically
   - Pattern recognition algorithms deployed

5.2. **Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)**
   - Filed with Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU)
   - For transactions above ₦5,000,000 or equivalent
   - Within 7 days of detection

5.3. **High-Risk Indicators**
   - Large cash transactions
   - Rapid movement of funds
   - Transactions with high-risk jurisdictions
   - Inconsistent transaction patterns

#### 6. SANCTIONS SCREENING
- Screen customers against international sanctions lists
- OFAC (USA)
- EU Sanctions List
- UN Sanctions List
- Nigerian government watchlists

#### 7. RECORD KEEPING
- Customer identification records: 5 years after relationship ends
- Transaction records: 7 years
- Suspicious transaction reports: 10 years
- Training records: 3 years

#### 8. STAFF TRAINING
- Mandatory AML training for all employees
- Annual refresher courses
- Assessment and certification
- Updated training on new regulations

#### 9. RISK ASSESSMENT
- Annual risk assessment conducted
- Customer risk profiling
- Geographic risk assessment
- Product/service risk evaluation

#### 10. INTERNAL CONTROLS
- Dedicated AML compliance officer
- Independent audit function
- Regular internal reviews
- Whistleblower policy

#### 11. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS
